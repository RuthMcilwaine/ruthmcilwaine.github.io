Homepage: After

Project Overview

McKinnon Netball Club required a simple, easy-to-manage website that could be updated by committee members with minimal technical experience. The goals were to improve usability, accessibility, performance, and search visibility, while maintaining the club’s branding in red, white, and blue

Constraints

  • Limited technical experience among volunteers; the site needed to be easy to update.
  • Budget restrictions meant relying on free or low-cost plugins.
  • The existing website used WordPress with Divi and WooCommerce, so changes needed to work with the current setup.
  • Live site actively used by members, requiring minimal disruption.

These factors required careful planning for every change, ensuring that technical decisions were safe and the committee remained confident in the process.

My Role & Delivery

  • Provided ongoing volunteer consulting, guiding the committee on website management and UX improvements.
  • Installed and configured plugins to simplify content management:
    • TablePress → Editable tables for timetables and results.
    • Adminimize → Simplified the WordPress dashboard for non-technical users.
  • Completed LinkedIn Learning courses on **SEO** and **WCAG 2.2 accessibility standards** to inform improvements and ensure best practices were followed.
  • Optimized images, page caching, and site structure to improve load times and overall user experience.
  • Updated WooCommerce product management with a plugin to allow straightforward price and stock updates.
  • Created documentation for the committee on how to manage content, forms, tables, and images.
  • Monitored performance, accessibility, and SEO with Lighthouse and analytics tools.

Key Decisions

  • Use WordPress over a custom SPA: Ensured long-term manageability for non-technical volunteers.

  • Performance & Speed Enhancements: W3 Total Cache, lazy loading, and optimized large images to reduce page load times.

  • SEO Improvements:
    • Yoast SEO for meta descriptions and structured data.
    • Descriptive permalinks / slugs (e.g., /about-us/) for clarity and keyword relevance.
    • Redirection plugin to handle URL changes safely, accommodating visitors who had bookmarked old URLs and ensuring Google indexing picked up the new links.
    • Sitemap.xml generated and submitted to Google for improved search indexing.

  • Content Editing Simplicity: TablePress and Adminimize to reduce complexity for non-technical users.

  • Maintain Divi Design: Preserved visual consistency while improving functionality.

  • Accessibility Focus: Added descriptive alt text, adjusted navigation menus, and checked ARIA roles.

Outcome

  • Performance: Lighthouse score improved from 59 → 87.
  • Accessibility: Improved from 75 → 78 through alt text, navigation adjustments, and ARIA checks.
  • SEO: Improved from 69 → 85 via descriptive URLs, safe redirects, meta descriptions, sitemap.xml submission, and alt text.
  • Committee members can now easily update pages, tables, images, and WooCommerce products without technical support.
  • The site is faster, more accessible, and easier to maintain, creating a better experience for club members and visitors alike.
  • Real-world impact: The committee were relieved they didn’t need to commission a new website — they had assumed a rebuild was necessary because the site felt slow, but the optimizations resolved the issues efficiently
